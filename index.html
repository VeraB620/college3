Why Computer Science?

     Computer science is one physical art of the science world. It is a blend of logic, creativity, and math that makes the field so intriguing. Also, one can physically see the results, its is not theoretical. Since the world had developed into one dependent on technology and since technology has advanced the world, computer science is the perfect way to help change the world. With computer science, I can choose exactly how I want to use it; I could use it in any field.
     Aside from wanting to do computer science, I would love to help encourage women to join the STEM fields. Sadly, there are less women in the science and mathematics fields, especially engineering, than males. In my first computer programming class, I was the only female. I think this is because women still feel inferior to men. It is important to encourage females to go into the field for ever evolving fields need a constant pair of fresh new eyes.

My whole life I have been traveling. I have been all over Europe. I also have been to Mexico, the Virgin Islands, China, and Canada. Travel is important to immerse yourself in a new culture. Chinese

My mom forced me to take Chinese my freshman year of high school, but I am glad she did. Chinese is an amazing language. The characters are beautiful, and each one has a story, like the character for home is a pig under a roof (thus home is where you can be a pig). Chinese has opened me up to the very cool culture of China. I was even able to go on my school's China trip; it was a huge culture change. I am now a co-president of my schools Chinese Cultural Society. We do activities (like movies, cook dumpling, tea ceremonies, visit China Town) to learn more about the culture. I am glad to help students learn more about the ancient world of China.
I also have received 2nd place in the Valparaiso University's Fifth Annual High School Chinese Speech Contest.

Learning

In biomechanics, a three day class, we designed a new scuba suit inspired from animals from the Shedd Aquarium and from the bio-mechanics exhibit in the Field Museum.

We got to meet amazing coders, like the creators of Cards Against Humanities and the head of Google in Chicago. It was an amazing experience, with lasting friendships. Now I can't stop coding!
